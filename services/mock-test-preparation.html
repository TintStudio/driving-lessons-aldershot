What to Expect During a Mock Test

A mock driving test closely replicates the real DVSA test. Learners will drive a full test route, encounter typical traffic situations, and perform all manoeuvres required during the actual exam. The instructor acts as the examiner, scoring performance and providing detailed feedback afterwards.

During a mock test in Aldershot, learners can expect to practise junctions, roundabouts, dual carriageways, and residential streets, as well as parallel parking, reversing around a corner, and other test manoeuvres. Independent driving exercises are included to simulate real exam conditions.

After the mock test, the instructor reviews performance in detail, highlighting strengths and areas that need improvement. This feedback allows learners to focus on specific skills before the actual test, ensuring that each subsequent lesson is productive and targeted.

Mock tests also help learners manage test-day nerves by creating familiarity with the procedure and environment. By repeating these simulated exams, learners gain confidence, reduce anxiety, and enter the real driving test well-prepared.
